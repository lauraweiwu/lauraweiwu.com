The historical roads were digitized from Shell Atlases in 1951 and 1956 hosted by the David Rumsey Historical Map Collection. I started with a modern shapefile of secondary roads (non-Interstates) and edited them to align with the historical maps. Major roads used for identification are labeled with the FRC_DESC variable under the value "Major Road Less Important than a Freeway", while all other roads enter only into the commute time calculations. Other variables in the dataframe should be ignored. 71 CBSAs are included in the shapefile (noted in the file List of CBSAs).

The planned Interstate Highways were digitized from Yellow Book (General Location of National System of Interstate Highways) maps in 1955. 96 CBSAs are included in the shapefile (noted in the file List of CBSAs). 100 cities have corresponding Yellow Book maps, but some (such as Dallas and Fort Worth) are within the same CBSA.

The national Interstate highway plan was digitized from a Public Roads Administration map in 1947. The Yellow Book maps are combined with the 1947 national plan in the shapefile, so this dataset spans the whole country rather than only the 96 CBSAs in the Yellow Book maps.

The rays follow the Yellow Book maps and connect intermediate cities/towns along the plans using straight-line rays. 96 CBSAs are included in the shapefile (noted in the file List of CBSAs).
